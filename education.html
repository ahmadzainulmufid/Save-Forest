What is Deforestation?

Deforestation refers to the clearing, destruction, or removal of forests by human activities, often to make way for agriculture, livestock, logging, mining, or urban development. It results in the conversion of forested land into non-forest uses.

Deforestation Rates

The rate of global forest loss has reached millions of hectares annually, posing severe ecological and environmental consequences. This phenomenon contributes to biodiversity decline, disrupts ecological balance, increases atmospheric carbon concentrations through reduced carbon sequestration, and accelerates global climate change. Furthermore, widespread deforestation affects hydrological cycles and undermines the socio-economic stability of indigenous and local communities who depend on forest ecosystems.

Social Impact

Deforestation severely affects local and indigenous communities by disrupting their livelihoods, diminishing access to essential resources, and threatening their cultural heritage and traditional knowledge systems. Moreover, it increases their socio-economic vulnerability, exposes them to land conflicts, and accelerates the loss of biodiversity crucial for their daily survival.

Main Impact

Biodiversity Loss

Deforestation destroys habitats and accelerates species extinction.

Climate Disruption

Forest loss contributes to CO₂ buildup and global warming.

Water Cycle Damage

Fewer trees disturb rainfall patterns and increase drought risk.

Human Impact

Local communities lose access to food, medicine, and livelihoods.
